Rural customers who connected to the grid in 2009

The first block of text will desribe the 2009 data: consumption rises rapidly within the first few months of connection, then essentially plateaus after a household has been connected for two years.

Rural customers who connected to the grid in 2010-2015

Customers who have connected to the grid more recently have plateaued at diminishing levels of consumption.

Two years after their initial connections, the median rural households that were connected in 2009 and 2010 were consistently consuming over 30 kilowatt-hours a month, yet the median households that were connected in 2011 and 2012 only consumed 25 kilowatt-hours a month after a similar period of access to electricity.

Note how this pattern holds for upper-quartile households as well.
